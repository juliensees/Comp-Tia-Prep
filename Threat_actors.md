### Threat Actors

- Nation-state is also known as an Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)

- unskilled attacker or "script kiddie"
      - less to worry about, but could still cause damage
- hacktivist - someone driven by ethical, philosophical differences, social/political cause
  
- organized crime
      - ransomware gangs, extortion, blackmail, identity theft
      - high skill level, and high level of funding

- insider threat
    - disgruntled or uneducated employees (malicious or simply incompetence)
 
- shadow IT
      - and installation of IT systems or software without permission of IT department
          - could be trying to be helpful, but could circumvent policy/undermine security controls

### Threat Vectors

* Social Engineering Attacks

    * message based attacks. 
        - email (phishing)   
        - SMS or text message   
        - chat - direct messages. 
    * Voice-based attacks.  
        - aka "vishing" or "voice phishing".  
        - trying to talk someone into doing something they normally wouldn't. 
     
* File-based attacks. 

    - malware through a downloaded file or email attachment  
    - fileless malware is another form, where the file deletes itself after doing the damage. 
    - images can use STEGANOGRAPHY - hiding within pixels. 
 
* Removable media

    - mass storage devices. 
    - data can be exfiltrated from an org through a removable media. 
    - malware can be introduced through removable media. 
    - "airgapped" systems can be compromised through removable media (STUXNET)  
 
* Vulnerable software

    - software can have security flaws baked in accidentally or maliciously
        - ALWAYS PATCH!
     
* Unsupported systems and apps

    - any system connected to an org's network should be identified and monitored  
          - CAN SCAN WITH CLIENT-BASED (AGENT-BASED) OR AGENTLESS SCANNING TO FIND THESE AND REMOVE. 

* Insecure networks

    - wired networks with open public cat5 ports  
    - wireless networks  
          - lack of wireless security (transmitting beyond confines of the facility). 
    - bluetooth. 
        - disable bluetooth if not needed. 
        - monitor these connections because it can be an attack vector  
        - bluetooth gives out a signal that tracks your movement (same as google maps/gps). 
     
* Open ports

    - any point where data can connect to an internal service is a potential point of attack. 
    - any open ports on a host or firewall is a potential vulnerability. 
    - all unecessary, uneeded services should be disabled and their ports closed. 
    - only required ports should be open on a firewall. 
 
* Default credentials

    - admin: admin. 
    - never leave the default settings on a system (there are many hacker systems to detect default)  
 
* Supply chain attacks

    - a cyberattack where an attacker goes for a less secure system within the supply chain. 
    - supply chain consists of:  
          - managed service providers (MSP). 
          - vendors. 
          - suppliers.
